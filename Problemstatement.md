Problem Statement 12: WhiteNet – A Parallel Identity Network 
Theme: CyberTech 
Objective: 
To design and prototype WhiteNet, a parallel identity-first network layer built on top of existing internet 
infrastructure, where every participant is cryptographically verified and assigned a certified, authority
backed IPv6 address — creating a Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA), high-trust environment purpose-built 
for critical digital services such as banking, e-governance, national identity systems, and healthcare. 
Problem Description: 
The modern internet was built for connectivity, not accountability. Critical services like banking portals, 
government platforms, healthcare systems, and digital identity infrastructure operate on a public internet that 
was never designed to verify who is actually on the other side of a connection.This fundamental anonymity 
has given rise to a growing crisis of trust — deepfake-driven KYC fraud, ghost beneficiaries in welfare systems, 
unauthorised access to medical records, and large-scale credential theft — all of which exploit the fact that 
the existing internet places no structural obligation on a user to prove their identity. What is needed is a 
governed, verified, and accountable network where identity is not a feature bolted on top, but the very 
foundation of participation. Just as the darknet was purpose-built for anonymity, WhiteNet must be purpose
built for verified identity — managed not by a single authority, but by a distributed community governance 
body modelled after ICANN, ensuring no single point of control or failure. 
Core Features: 
• Security Resilience Built-in resistance against spoofing, man-in-the-middle attacks, Sybil 
attacks, and certificate forgery through end-to-end PKI or blockchain-backed certificate chain 
validation. 
• Certified IPv6 Identity Issuance Every node on WhiteNet is assigned a unique IPv6 address 
cryptographically bound to a government or institution-issued digital certificate, making 
impersonation and spoofing structurally impossible. 
• Integration with secure protocols (e.g., TLS 1.3, DNSSEC, and VPN tunneling) 
• Trust-Based Routing Protocol A routing layer that only forwards packets from verified nodes. 
ensuring every hop across the network is authenticated and traceable to a certified identity. 
• Decentralised Governance A community-managed governing body responsible for certificate 
authority oversight, IPv6 address allocation, policy amendments, and dispute resolution — 
transparent, distributed, and independent. 
• Existing Infrastructure Compatibility WhiteNet operates as an overlay on top of current 
internet infrastructure, requiring no hardware changes and allowing institutions to onboard 
progressively without disrupting existing services. 
• Sector-Specific Application Support Native support for high-stakes verticals including 
banking transactions, government service portals, national digital identity, and healthcare record 
access — where verified individual identity is non-negotiable.
